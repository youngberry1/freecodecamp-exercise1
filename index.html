Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a popular and flavorful West African dish made primarily from rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices. It is known for its vibrant red color, which comes from the tomatoes and often includes ingredients like bell peppers, garlic, and ginger. The dish is typically cooked in a one-pot method, where the rice absorbs the rich, savory tomato sauce, creating a deliciously aromatic meal. Jollof rice is often served with grilled or fried chicken, beef, fish, or vegetables, making it a festive and versatile dish enjoyed across many countries, particularly in Nigeria, Ghana, and other parts of West Africa. Each region has its own variations, but it's always a staple at parties, celebrations, and family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Bell peppers

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Rinse the rice under cold water to remove excess starch.
    • Set aside to drain.
  2. Make the Tomato Blend:
    • Blend the tomatoes, bell peppers, onions, garlic, and ginger together until smooth.
  3. Fry the Tomato Paste (optional):
    • In a large pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the tomato paste and fry for about 3â€“5 minutes.
  4. Cook the Tomato Mixture:
    • Add the blended tomato mixture into the pot with the oil. Stir well.
  5. Season the Sauce:
    • Add thyme, curry powder, paprika, bay leaf, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper to the tomato mixture.
  6. Add the Rice:
    • Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir until it is well coated with the tomato sauce.
  7. Cook the Rice:
    • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot.
  8. Allow to Rest:
    • Once the rice is cooked, let it sit, covered, for 10 minutes.
  9. Serve:
    • Fluff the rice with a fork and serve it hot with your favorite protein and vegetables.

Enjoy your delicious Jollof rice!
